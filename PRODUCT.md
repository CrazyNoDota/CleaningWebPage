# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Busy urban professionals in Astana, Kazakhstan. Mid-to-high income, typically booking on mobile. They trust platforms that show real reviews, transparent pricing, and vetted cleaners. They are inviting a stranger into their home, so every friction point raises their anxiety.

Secondary: families and office managers, but the primary journey is the individual apartment booking.

## Product Purpose

A cleaning service booking platform serving Astana. Customers browse cleaners, see tiered pricing, and book a cleaning in as few steps as possible. Success looks like a completed booking, a confirmed appointment, and a customer who books again.

## Brand Personality

Warm, Reliable, Local. Friendly and approachable: booking a trusted neighbor, not calling a corporation. The product should feel human and Kazakhstani, not like a generic tech startup that happens to be deployed in Central Asia.

Reference: top.kz — structured, clear, built for Kazakh users without pretending to be something imported.

## Anti-references

- Cheap local classifieds: OLX / Avito density, clutter, low-trust visual language.
- Corporate cold blue: heavy navy, bank-like rigidity. Feels like a utility bill, not a service you'd recommend to a friend.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through clarity.** The user is letting someone into their home. Remove every ambiguity — names, photos, ratings, pricing, timing — before they have to ask.
2. **Mobile-first completions.** The booking flow must be frictionless at one thumb's reach. Desktop is secondary.
3. **Local warmth over global polish.** Feel like an Astana business. Not a VC pitch deck that speaks Russian.
4. **Hierarchy earns attention.** Busy professionals skim. Typography and spacing must do the filtering work so they don't have to.
5. **Honest pricing, up front.** Prices visible before the booking flow. No "get a quote" mystery.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Russian / Kazakh / English — all three locales must be legible and functional. Reduced-motion support preferred given the mobile-first audience.
